Meet the Team

HydraTrack was built by four Computer Science students at the University of Utah as part of the CS 4500 Senior Design Capstone.

Wynter Kim

Team Lead · Architecture · AI Feature

I'm a Computer Science student at the University of Utah with a particular interest in software architecture and data structures — the underlying decisions that determine how well a system is organized, scales, and holds together over time. I enjoy the process of building things from the ground up and thinking carefully about how the pieces fit together.

As team lead of HydraTrack, I was responsible for the overall application architecture, database design, and the project's AI-powered hydration analysis feature. I have a growing interest in AI-assisted development and the ways modern tools are reshaping how engineers approach problem-solving — an area I find both practically useful and genuinely exciting to explore.

Outside of academics, I enjoy gaming, reading, and the occasional workout. I'm pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science and am interested in software engineering roles focused on backend systems and application architecture.
